FAQ

Common Questions

Are the crystals real?

Yes! Every bracelet is made with genuine crystal beads — never glass or plastic imitations. You can feel the difference.

What does it mean to cleanse and charge crystals?

Cleansing removes any stagnant or negative energy the crystals may have absorbed. Charging sets a positive intention into them. I use a combination of sage, sound, and intention to prepare each piece before it ships.

Can I request a custom bracelet?

Absolutely! Tell me your intention — protection, love, abundance, healing — or specific crystals you want, and I'll create something unique for you. Just use the contact form below.

What size are the bracelets?

All bracelets are made on high-quality elastic cord and stretch to fit most wrists comfortably (6-8 inches). If you need a specific size, let me know when ordering.

How should I care for my crystal bracelet?

To keep your bracelet's energy fresh, you can cleanse it at home by placing it on a selenite charging plate or next to a selenite wand overnight — selenite is one of the few crystals that cleanses and recharges other stones naturally. You can also use moonlight (especially full moons), running water, or sage smoke. Store on a soft surface when not wearing, and avoid wearing in the shower or pool.

Do you ship nationwide?

Yes! All orders ship within the US via USPS. Each bracelet is carefully packaged with a crystal info card describing the properties of your stones.

What is Reiki?

Reiki is a gentle, non-invasive form of energy healing. As a Certified Reiki Master, I channel universal life force energy through my hands to help clear energetic blockages, reduce stress, and promote deep relaxation and healing. Sessions are available in-person in Culver City or as distance healing.

How does distance Reiki work?

Reiki energy is not limited by physical space. During a distance session, I connect with your energy field remotely — you simply relax in a comfortable space while I work. Many clients find distance sessions just as powerful as in-person ones.
